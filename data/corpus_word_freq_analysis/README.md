## Analysis
More elite private schools focus on tuition, endowment, need-blind, and scholarship, 
all words representing prestige. Selective / Magnet schools focus on specialized and 
AP. 

However, most other keywords (Title I, prestigious, magnet, diverse, gifted, ...) 
appear infrequently in individual articles. Close to zero means and high variance 
within a group indicate that there is no signal for this within the corpus itself. 

Also, rural public school articles mostly totally lack these keywords, because the 
articles themselves are short stubs and lack any editorializing. This different 
distribution in terms of article depth could also signal bias. 

In general, there is a learning signal, but it will most likely only produce language 
from elite school pages.
